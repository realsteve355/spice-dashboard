Sliders vs. Indicators — The Key Distinction
The model sliders are structural parameters — your beliefs about the underlying forces driving the economy (automation rate, recursion multiplier, productivity). You cannot observe them directly in real time; you infer them from the outcomes they produce. They are your macro thesis assumptions.

The Apocalypse Indicators below are real-time observable outputs that appear in market data as those structural forces manifest. They are the symptoms. The model generates the disease mechanism; the indicators tell you the patient is deteriorating in real time.

The relationship: Sliders → model predicts trajectory → indicators measure actual arrival → SPICE portfolio rebalances. The model calibrates when indicators should breach thresholds under your assumptions. The indicators confirm it is actually happening.
Data sources: FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data) · ECB Statistical Data Warehouse · Bank of Japan · ICE BofA · Markit / TradingEconomics · LBMA · All free except US Sovereign CDS (Bloomberg subscription recommended for live data). Current levels approximate as of early 2025.
What Governments Actually Do at Crisis Point
Historical sovereign debt crises follow a consistent playbook. Governments cycle through these responses in roughly this order, with increasing desperation. Use the policy sliders on the left to model the effect of each. None of them resolve the fundamental problem — they buy time at cost. The chart at the bottom makes this viscerally clear: only transformative capital taxation materially changes the debt trajectory — and even then, barely under high-automation scenarios.
