Reinstatement RateReinstatement Rate
The percentage of displaced tasks replaced by new human roles. History suggests this has always been ~100% over long periods — but never at this speed.

Low reinstatement is the key departure from historical precedent. Previous automation (looms, tractors) created new jobs. AI may not.

AI ProductivityAI Productivity Multiplier
How much more output one automated task produces versus the human it replaced.

1× = no gain. 4× = SPICE base case (AI does the work of four workers). 6× = singularity territory.

Higher productivity grows GDP — but that growth flows to asset owners, not the tax base. The Output Index rises; real wages and revenues fall.

This is the mechanism that makes the Collision paradoxical: growth and crisis simultaneously.4.0×

RecursionRecursion Multiplier
The annual acceleration of automation — AI improving AI. Each year's automation rate is multiplied by this factor.

1.0 = no acceleration. 1.20 = 20% faster each year (SPICE base). 1.25 = near-singularity territory.

Small differences compound dramatically over 10 years. 1.20 vs 1.25 is the difference between manageable disruption and civilisational transformation.

This is the most uncertain parameter in the model — and the most consequential.1.20×

Primary DeficitPrimary Deficit

Capital TaxCapital Tax Rate
The effective tax rate applied to AI profits and investment income. The policy lever most discussed as a response to AI displacement.

Currently ~8% effective rate in most economies (nominal rates are higher but avoidance is endemic). 25-30% is the ambitious reform target.

Why it doesn't fully solve the problem: capital taxes only help if profits are taxable domestically — see the Domestic Capture slider. Offshore AI profits (Silicon Valley serving the UK) cannot be taxed by Westminster.

High capital tax + low domestic capture = revenue illusion.8%

Domestic AI CaptureDomestic AI Capture
The percentage of AI productivity gains that are taxable in this jurisdiction. The UK's most severe structural disadvantage.

US: 70-90% (Silicon Valley is domestic). UK/Europe: 20-50% (profits go to US firms, taxed in Delaware or Ireland). This is why non-US sovereigns face a double bind: lose the labour tax base AND cannot tax the capital that replaced it.

Even aggressive capital tax reform fails without domestic capture — you cannot tax profits booked in San Francisco.

This single parameter explains why the UK fiscal position is structurally worse than headline numbers suggest.50%

The paradox: QE suppresses bond yields (good for debt service) but erodes fiat credibility (accelerates crypto exit).

Wage Income Share of GDP
The fraction of all national income paid as wages and salaries — versus profits, dividends and rents going to asset owners owners.

62% is the approximate post-war average. It has been drifting down since the 1980s and sits around 58–60% today.

Why it matters: income tax, National Insurance and VAT all follow the wage bill. When wage income share falls, government revenues deteriorate structurally — regardless of what policy does. Below 55%, the fiscal position begins to unravel. Below 40%, welfare costs exceed what the remaining tax base can fund.

Thresholds: <58% warning · <50% stress · <40% severe · <28% collapse

A falling real wage index while the Output Index rises is the defining signature of the Collision — productivity gains are real, but they flow to capital, not workers.

Sovereign Bond Yield
The interest rate the market demands to lend to the government. Rises with fiscal stress as investors demand a risk premium.

Key levels:
<5.5% — Elevated but manageable
5.5–7.5% — Stress: markets pricing meaningful default risk

Under YCC: the cap is artificial. The suppressed premium gets monetised instead — the crisis moves from the bond market to the currency.

Structural Unemployment
Unemployment driven by permanent displacement — jobs that AI and robotics have eliminated and that reinstatement has not replaced. This is not cyclical unemployment that recovers with growth.

Why it compounds the fiscal crisis:
Every displaced worker simultaneously removes tax revenue and adds welfare cost. At 15% structural unemployment, the fiscal drag is severe. At 25%+, it overwhelms the remaining tax base.

Thresholds:
<8% — Above normal, drag beginning
8–12% — Elevated, political instability risk
12–20% — Social crisis, historical precedent for government change
>20% — Depression-level, structural collapse

Unlike 1930s unemployment, this cannot be solved by stimulus — the jobs do not come back.

🏛 RESERVE CURRENCY BUFFER — US EXCEPTIONALISM
The US dollar's reserve status gives the US a genuine but finite buffer. Global demand for Treasuries as collateral is structural — banks, central banks, and repo markets require them. The US can sustain higher debt/GDP ratios than Greece or Italy before triggering equivalent market panic, probably 150–170% vs. 120–130% for non-reserve economies. This is a difference of timing, not outcome.
⚡ WHEN THE US BREAKS — NOBODY IS SPARED
A US Treasury bond crisis is categorically different from any other sovereign crisis because Treasuries are the global financial system's collateral layer. Every repo agreement, every bank's capital buffer, every central bank's reserve holds Treasuries. A US yield shock triggers global margin calls, liquidity seizure, and simultaneous repricing of every other sovereign — within hours. The buffer delays the crisis. It does not contain it when it arrives.

Sliders vs. Indicators — The Key Distinction
The model sliders are structural parameters — your beliefs about underlying forces (automation rate, recursion). You cannot observe them directly; you infer them from outcomes. They are your macro thesis assumptions.

The Apocalypse Indicators are real-time observable outputs that appear in market data as those structural forces manifest. They are the symptoms. The model generates the disease mechanism; the indicators tell you the patient is deteriorating in real time.

The relationship: Sliders → model predicts trajectory → indicators measure actual arrival → SPICE portfolio rebalances. The model calibrates when indicators should breach thresholds under your assumptions. The indicators confirm it is actually happening.
