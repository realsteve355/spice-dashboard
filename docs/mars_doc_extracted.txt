MARS COLONY ECONOMY
Token & Governance System
A complete reference for the S-token and V-token economy, the roles of citizens, companies, MCC, and the Fisc, and the rules of property ownership.
Founding Philosophy
The post-scarcity premise
Due to enormous advances in AI, automation, and robotics, the Mars Colony exists in a
post-scarcity world where basic human needs can be met at extremely low cost.
Every citizen is provided with a universal income sufficient to pay for all services
they use, with enough left over to lead a minimally fulfilling life.
"Money" is still needed to drive efficiency, to manage the consumption of scarce
resources, and to incentivise enterprise and endeavour.
The floor of basic social support is guaranteed for all,
and the opportunity ceiling is infinite.
Part 1 — The Token System
1.1  S-Tokens — The Spending Currency
S-tokens are the colony's medium of exchange. They are issued monthly by the Fisc to every citizen as Universal Basic Income and expire at the end of each month.
Property
Detail
Issued by
The Fisc — automatically, on the 1st of each month
Amount
1,000 S-tokens per adult citizen per month (fixed)
Expiry
Midnight on the last day of the month — unused S-tokens are destroyed
Purpose
Medium of exchange — pay MCC bills, buy goods and services, purchase company shares, convert to V-tokens
Inflation
None — the monthly hard reset destroys any unspent supply, preventing accumulation
Anti-hoarding
S-tokens cannot be carried forward. Spending or converting is the only rational choice
Children
Receive full UBI from birth, managed by a registered guardian
The monthly expiry is the system's anti-inflation mechanism. Because the total S-token supply resets to zero each month regardless of what was spent, there is no mechanism by which unspent money can chase a fixed supply of goods. The "circuit breaker" makes inflation structurally impossible in S-tokens.
1.2  V-Tokens — Permanent Savings
V-tokens are the colony's store of value. They never expire, accumulate over a lifetime, and are the primary mechanism by which citizens build lasting wealth above their UBI baseline.
Property
Detail
Created by
Converting S-tokens (citizens and companies) — the Fisc never issues V-tokens directly
Citizen cap
Up to 200 S-tokens per month may be converted to V-tokens (20% of UBI)
Company cap
Up to 20% of net monthly S-token earnings may be converted to V-tokens
Expiry
Never — V-tokens are permanent
Redemption
Can be redeemed back to S-tokens at any time at full face value
Uses
Redeem to S-tokens, purchase company shares, purchase physical assets, pay for services
Dividends
V-tokens received as equity dividends are uncapped and never expire
Inheritance
Transferred automatically to registered next of kin by the Fisc on death
Seizure
Cannot be seized by any colonial authority under any circumstances

1.4  Is the System Inflationary?
The system is non-inflationary in S-tokens by design. It is conditionally non-inflationary in V-tokens.
S-tokens: total supply resets to zero every month. There is no mechanism for inflation.
V-tokens: the total pool grows over time as citizens and companies save. If productive capacity grows at the same rate as the V-token pool, purchasing power is stable.
If V-tokens accumulate faster than colony output grows, V-token purchasing power falls. This is the system's only inflation risk — and it is mitigated by the 20% monthly conversion cap.
The simulation monitors V-token concentration (top holder as % of pool) as the primary early warning indicator.

Part 3 — The Citizen
3.1  Rights
1,000 S-tokens issued unconditionally on the 1st of each month, for life — cannot be withheld, reduced, or means-tested
One non-transferable MCC share — one vote in MCC governance, issued on adulthood, retired on death
Full property rights — the right to own S-tokens, V-tokens, company equity, physical assets, and land rights, all recorded and enforced by the Fisc
3.2  Obligations
Pay the monthly MCC bill from UBI — itemised, variable, based on actual consumption of metered services
No other obligations — no tax, no mandatory contribution, no minimum activity
3.3  Compensation — Two Mechanisms
Citizens earn in two ways, which are fundamentally different in character:
Mechanism
S-token payment vs V-token dividend
S-token payment
Payment for a specific service or task — received immediately, expires at month end if unspent
V-token dividend
Monthly share of company profits — permanent savings, does not expire, effectively wages for ongoing contribution
Who receives S
Any citizen providing a one-off service to another citizen or company
Who receives V
Equity holders in a company, in proportion to their shareholding, paid monthly
Example (S)
A plumber fixes a seal — paid 150 S-tokens on the day
Example (V)
An equity holder receives 340 V-tokens at month end from the Engineering Guild
The V-token dividend is the system's equivalent of wages for ongoing work. The difference from a traditional wage is that the recipient is also an owner — they share in the company's risk and reward, not just its labour cost. In a post-AI economy where robots do the capital-intensive work, human contribution is intellectual and directorial, and equity reflects that.
Part 4 — The Company
4.1  Formation
Any citizen may found a company by registering it with the Fisc
Registration is administrative — it is required for access to the payment system, not a licence to operate
No exclusive territories, no state protection from competition
Two or more founding citizens share equity at formation — each receives a percentage stake registered on the blockchain
A company that cannot cover its costs fails — there is no subsidy or bailout
4.2  Monthly Token Rules
During the month
Earns S-tokens from citizens and other companies in exchange for goods and services
Pays for materials and services from other companies in S-tokens
S-tokens accumulate in the company's Fisc-registered wallet throughout the month
At month end
Up to 20% of net monthly S-tokens convert to V-tokens via the Fisc
Converted V-tokens are distributed immediately as dividends to equity holders in proportion to shareholding
All remaining S-tokens expire — the company starts each month from zero
The company retains no S-token balance between months
4.3  Equity and Shares
Shares represent fractional ownership of a company's future earnings, registered on the blockchain
May be bought and sold between any parties at freely agreed prices in S-tokens or V-tokens
Share transfers are atomic — payment and ownership change simultaneously on chain
May be inherited — transferred to registered next of kin on the owner's death
May be granted by a founder to a contributor in lieu of or alongside S-token payment
May not be pledged as collateral — the system does not support debt instruments
All shareholdings are publicly visible on the blockchain — no anonymous ownership
Part 5 — The Mars Colony Company (MCC)
5.1  What MCC Is
MCC is a company — governed by the same financial rules as every other company — with one difference: every adult citizen owns exactly one equal non-transferable share. It exists to provide the essential infrastructure that cannot safely be left to market competition.
MCC core facts
Type:           Company — same token rules as all other companies
Owner:          Every adult citizen holds one equal, non-transferable share
Board:          Elected annually by citizen shareholders
Revenue:        Itemised metered bills (citizens) + infrastructure levies (companies)
Pricing:        Set by the board as a commercial decision — not by citizen vote
Profit share:   Determined by the board, disclosed on blockchain, judged at election
Mandate:        Fixed in the founding constitution — changeable only by 80% referendum
Constraint:     May not compete commercially with cottage industries
5.2  What MCC Provides and Bills For
Every MCC service is metered where technically possible. Bills are itemised and variable — based on actual consumption. The target average is ~200 S-tokens per citizen per month (20% of UBI).

5.3  The Board
The board manages MCC with full commercial discretion — setting prices, managing costs, determining its own profit share. It is accountable for all three through a single annual election.

The board sets its own profit share. This sounds alarming until you understand the accountability mechanism: every revenue, cost, and board earnings figure is published in real time on the blockchain. Any citizen can inspect MCC's complete financial position at any moment. If costs spike without justification, the Fisc triggers an automatic recall vote.
Part 6 — The Fisc
6.1  What the Fisc Is
The Fisc is the colony's fully automated financial institution. It is not a company and it is not part of MCC. It is a constitutional utility — created by the founding charter, governed by rules not by people, operated entirely by software. No board, no employees, no profit motive.
The Fisc cannot be lobbied, bribed, pressured, or voted out. It executes monetary rules with perfect consistency — the same rule for every citizen and every company, every time.
6.2  What the Fisc Does
Issues 1,000 S-tokens to every citizen wallet on the 1st of each month
Generates and delivers itemised MCC bills from metered usage data
Processes all S-to-V token conversions for citizens and companies
Enforces month-end S-token expiry across all wallets simultaneously
Operates the colonial blockchain — the authoritative record of all ownership
Processes inheritance transfers automatically on registered death
Registers new companies and issues company wallet addresses
Records all share transfers, asset registrations, and property right assignments
Publishes all transaction data publicly — the blockchain is readable by any citizen
Locks board V-token conversions during declared emergencies
6.3  Why the Fisc Must Be Separate from MCC
This is a foundational design principle. If the Fisc were part of MCC, the board that sets its own profit share would also control the institution that issues currency and enforces monetary rules. That creates an unacceptable conflict of interest.
The Fisc serves all participants equally — citizens, companies, and MCC
Constitutional protection #10: the Fisc may not be placed under MCC or company control
No elected body, no board, no company has authority over the Fisc's operation
Changes to Fisc rules require a blockchain referendum of 80% of all registered citizens
Part 7 — Property Rights
7.1  The Blockchain as Legal Reality
All significant ownership in the colony is recorded on the Fisc's blockchain. Ownership is not enforced primarily by physical force — it is enforced by cryptographic certainty. If it is not on the blockchain, it is not legally owned.
A robot whose registered owner differs from the person attempting to operate it will not respond. A share transfer that does not appear on chain has not occurred. A surface land claim without a blockchain entry has no legal standing.
7.2  The Four Registries
The Fisc maintains four registries that together constitute the complete record of ownership in the colony.
Registry
Records
Wallets
S-token balance, V-token balance, full transaction history for every citizen and company
Shares
Fractional company ownership, transfer history, monthly dividend history per equity holder
Assets
Physical asset ownership, type, description, acquisition date, transfer history
Rights
Habitat rights, surface land Harberger claims, licence rights, expiry dates where applicable
7.3  Physical Asset Ownership
Registration threshold: any asset valued above 500 S-tokens, above 50kg, or possessing autonomous AI capability
Below threshold: personal items — possession implies ownership, no registration required
Registered to a citizen ID or company ID — both parties sign any transfer on chain
Unregistered assets above threshold: no legal protection and no Fisc recovery if stolen or disputed
MCC infrastructure — dome, life support, power, water plant — is owned by MCC collectively and cannot be privatised
7.4  Surface Land — Harberger Stewardship
Surface land on Mars is finite. The Harberger model ensures land is always held by the party with the most productive use for it, preventing speculative hoarding.
How Harberger stewardship works
1.  Every surface land claim is registered with the Fisc at a declared V-token value
set by the owner.
2.  The owner pays a monthly stewardship fee to MCC — a percentage of their
declared value. This funds MCC's cost of maintaining access rights.
3.  Anyone may purchase the land at the declared value at any time.
The owner cannot refuse. The transaction is atomic and on chain.
4.  The self-correcting incentive:
— Price too high: monthly fee is expensive. Idle land costs you.
— Price too low: someone buys it immediately.
— Price honestly: you pay a fair fee and keep the land unless
someone genuinely has better use for it.
5.  Harberger applies to surface land only. Habitat space inside the dome
is managed by MCC via habitation charges — not Harberger.
Part 8 — Constitutional Protections
The following rules require a blockchain referendum of 80% of all registered citizens to amend. They exist to protect the system from being dismantled by temporary majorities.
Protection
What it guarantees
1. UBI floor
UBI may not fall below 1,000 S-tokens per adult per month
2. UBI universality
UBI may not be conditional, means-tested, or withheld for any reason
3. MCC equal shareholding
Every citizen holds exactly one MCC share — no citizen holds more or fewer
4. MCC share non-transferability
MCC shares cannot be bought, sold, or inherited
5. MCC mandate
MCC may not compete commercially with cottage industries
6. V-token protection
No authority may confiscate V-tokens under any circumstances, including declared emergencies
7. Blockchain transparency
All ownership is publicly visible on the blockchain at all times
8. MCC asset protection
MCC infrastructure may not be privatised under any circumstances
9. Company freedom
No licence required to operate a company beyond Fisc registration
10. Fisc autonomy
The Fisc may not be placed under MCC or company control
Part 9 — Open Questions
The following design questions remain undecided as of Version 6.
The Harberger stewardship fee percentage — what % of declared value per month?
Exact definition of the physical asset registration threshold — 500 S / 50kg is a working assumption
First registration of unclaimed surface land — first come first served, or MCC-allocated?
Whether V-tokens may be used to pay MCC bills directly, or only S-tokens accepted
Minimum share unit — 0.01% is proposed
The automatic recall trigger — what cost spike threshold triggers a recall vote?
Board term length — annual election proposed, but should there be a minimum term?
The formal viability equation — the mathematical relationship between colony output, MCC costs, UBI level, and system stability — to be derived once the simulation is proven stable
V-token expiry over very long periods — whether dynastic concentration justifies eventual expiry
