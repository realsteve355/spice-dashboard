**1. The Investment Thesis**

> *Two irresistible forces are on a collision course. AI is structurally
> deflationary --- destroying jobs, compressing margins, concentrating
> gains in capital. Simultaneously, governments worldwide carry debt
> loads that can only be managed through sustained inflation.
> Governments need inflation. AI is preventing it. The resolution of
> this contradiction will be the defining financial event of the next
> decade.*

The portfolio is built to survive and profit from the most likely
resolution scenarios: financial repression, currency debasement, bond
market stress, capital controls, and a fundamental loss of confidence in
fiat-denominated assets.

Following the Hemingway pattern --- gradually, then all at once --- the
fund operates in two phases. Phase 1 (IRON token): accumulation during
the gradual deterioration. Phase 2 (SPICE token): crisis-era hard
currency when the sudden phase arrives.

**2. What This Portfolio Is --- and Is Not --- Hedging**

This is the most important distinction to understand before examining
the portfolio. The SPICE portfolio is not a conventional macro hedge. It
is built for a different category of risk, and this determines every
asset selection decision that follows.

**2.1 What We Are Not Hedging**

A standard defensive portfolio --- gold ETFs, TIPS, short-duration
bonds, equity puts, VIX exposure --- is designed to survive a recession,
a growth scare, or an equity market correction. These are cyclical
events within a functioning financial system. The instruments that
protect you in those scenarios work precisely because the system itself
survives them.

The following instruments, which appear in conventional macro hedge
portfolios, are explicitly excluded from SPICE and it is worth being
clear about why:

instrument that performs its function only while the entity you are
hedging against remains solvent and law-abiding fails precisely when you
need it most.

**2.2 What We Are Hedging**

SPICE is built for a different category of event --- one in which the
infrastructure of the financial system itself is impaired. The question
is not \'what goes up when equities go down?\' It is \'what retains
value when the system through which conventional assets are settled,
custodied, and redeemed is under structural stress?\'

**Detailed Rationale --- Gold**

PAXG (Paxos Gold) is the preferred instrument. Each token represents one
troy ounce of physical gold held in Brinks vaults in London, fully
audited and redeemable. Gold provides the traditional hard asset anchor
that performs across all four scenarios. In Scenario A it provides
stability and steady appreciation as macro stress builds. In Scenario B
it surges as confidence in fiat erodes. In Scenario C it benefits from
the acute safe-haven bid. In Scenario D it provides the stable base
while other positions activate sequentially. XAUT (Tether Gold) is the
secondary option. Neither is an ETF. Both represent direct on-chain
claims on allocated physical metal.

**Detailed Rationale --- Commodities**

Commodities have intrinsic value independent of any government\'s
promise. The basket is constructed around commodities with the most
compelling thesis-specific demand drivers, not general commodity
exposure:

-   Copper: Essential for electrification and AI infrastructure
    build-out. Supply constraints are already visible in mine production
    data. Demand is structurally inelastic.

-   Uranium / Cameco (CCJ): Nuclear power is the only energy source that
    can supply AI data centre demand at scale without carbon
    constraints. The nuclear revival is structural, not cyclical.

-   Silver: Combines monetary properties with significant industrial
    demand in electronics and solar. Historically outperforms gold in
    acute crisis phases.

-   Oil and gas (XOM/CVX synthetic): Energy security is inelastic in
    crisis. The energy transition narrative does not eliminate near-term
    oil and gas demand, particularly in scenarios of geopolitical
    stress.

-   Agricultural commodities: Food security premium in genuine crisis
    scenarios. Basic necessity demand is the definition of inelastic.

**Detailed Rationale --- AI Infrastructure**

This is the most deliberately paradoxical inclusion. The portfolio owns
the thing causing the disruption. The reasoning is that because AI is
causing the disruption and the disruption is irreversible and
accelerating, the picks-and-shovels of AI --- GPU manufacturers, data
centre operators, energy infrastructure providers --- are among the few
positions that appreciate throughout regardless of how the macro crisis
resolves. Whether governments inflate, repress, or face dislocation, AI
adoption continues. The Jevons Paradox that challenges the employment
deflation thesis actively supports the AI infrastructure investment
case: as AI inference became 900 times cheaper, total compute demand
rose. Infrastructure owners benefit from the volume expansion regardless
of which applications win.

the sudden phase. The critical design point, which distinguishes this
sleeve from a conventional macro hedge, is that it is structured around
bond market and sovereign stress events, not equity market corrections.

VIX instruments and equity put options are explicitly excluded. The VIX
measures US equity volatility and pays off in equity selloffs. A
sovereign debt crisis does not necessarily produce an immediate equity
selloff --- rates can spike and equities hold for a period as inflation
runs. The relevant volatility measure for this thesis is sovereign bond
market volatility (the MOVE index equivalent in DeFi), rates volatility
instruments, and commodity call options structured around the

**4.3 IRON-to-SPICE Switchover Trigger**

The compulsory conversion from IRON to SPICE tokens is triggered
algorithmically when a defined combination of macro stress indicators
are simultaneously breached. The trigger is objective, verifiable, and
resistant to manipulation.

All three of the following conditions must be simultaneously met:

-   Six or more macro stress indicators in crisis threshold territory
    simultaneously.

-   At least one G7 sovereign bond market showing CDS spreads above
    200bp.

-   Gold appreciation above 100% from fund inception NAV.

Note: The exact trigger definition requires a dedicated legal and
technical working session. The trigger must be encoded in the smart
contract and publicly disclosed in the whitepaper from day one.
Historical back-testing against prior sovereign stress events is
required to ensure the trigger is not tripped prematurely.

**5. Fee Structure**

**5.1 Management Fee**

1.5% annual management fee, accrued continuously by the smart contract.
Taken in the fund\'s denomination. No manual invoicing. Applies in both
Phase 1 (IRON) and Phase 2 (SPICE).

**5.2 Performance Fee**

A performance fee of 1.5% applies only to returns that exceed a
conventional 60/40 benchmark. If SPICE does not outperform a standard
balanced portfolio, no performance fee is charged. The honest statement:
if we don\'t beat a conventional portfolio, we don\'t take a performance
fee.

Note: Dual reporting is important. Returns should be shown both against
the 60/40 benchmark (for the performance fee calculation) and against
fiat purchasing power (for investor psychology). In a crisis scenario,
these will tell very different stories.

**5.3 Protocol and Gas Costs**

Network gas costs and underlying protocol fees are borne by the fund and
reflected in NAV rather than charged separately. Transaction batching is
the primary mechanism for managing these costs. Total estimated ongoing
cost of carry across all short and leveraged positions: approximately
1.5--3% per year of the active positions sleeve value.
