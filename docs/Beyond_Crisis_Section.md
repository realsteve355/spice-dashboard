## Beyond the Crisis: The Monetary Reset Question

### The SPICE Scope (2025-2035)

This simulation models the **crisis itself**—the collision between unsustainable sovereign debt, AI-driven deflation, and crypto-enabled capital flight. Our investment thesis is straightforward: **hedge the transition with hard assets** (Bitcoin, gold, sovereign bond shorts).

What comes AFTER the crisis is deliberately out of scope. The post-crisis monetary order may take decades to crystallize, and SPICE is not a bet on which system wins—it's a hedge against the current system breaking.

---

### Three Possible Endpoints (Post-2035)

**We make no prediction.** History shows reserve currency transitions resolve in unexpected ways. Here are three scenarios—all possible, none certain:

---

#### Scenario 1: The Bitcoin Standard (Permissionless Reset)

**Thesis:** Bitcoin becomes the global reserve currency because it's the only credible neutral option.

**The Logic:**

Bitcoin solves the post-crisis credibility problem:
- **Fairness:** Neither US nor China controls it (acceptable to all nations)
- **Discipline:** Can't be printed (prevents inflation, forces fiscal restraint)
- **Stability:** Removes currency arbitrage uncertainty (no more forex chaos)
- **Network effects:** Already adopted by 40-60% during crisis (path dependency)

**Historical Analog: The Gold Standard (1870s-1914)**

The gold standard wasn't imposed by force—countries **chose** it voluntarily:
- Why? Import credibility, attract capital, prevent government debasement
- How? Coordinated adoption over 10-15 years (Britain first, others followed)
- Duration? 40+ years until WWI suspended it

Bitcoin could follow the same pattern, but **harder to abandon** (no central authority to suspend in wartime).

**How It Could Happen:**

**2030-2033: Crisis Forces Hyperinflation**
- Sovereign debt crisis triggers money printing
- Inflation 15-25%, currency losing purchasing power daily
- Government restrictions on crypto fail (enforcement becomes futile)

**2033-2034: Political Capitulation**
- Citizens already using Bitcoin (40-60% adoption despite restrictions)
- Opposition parties campaign on "Legalize Bitcoin" platforms
- Democratic elections: crypto-friendly candidates win landslides
- **Key insight:** In democracies, voters demand sound money after hyperinflation

**2034-2035: Democratic Adoption**
- New governments make Bitcoin legal tender (El Salvador 2021 model, but post-crisis)
- US, UK, EU adopt within 12-18 months of each other
- Why? Already widespread use, political pressure, economic necessity

**2035-2038: Global Cascade**
- Once major economies adopt, trading partners follow (network effects)
- Central banks hold Bitcoin reserves (like gold pre-1971)
- International trade settles in Bitcoin (neutral, instant, no counterparty risk)
- Fiat currencies persist for domestic use, but pegged to Bitcoin (like gold standard)

**The Result: Bitcoin as Global Reserve**
- Governments can't print (forced fiscal discipline)
- Cross-border trade frictionless (no currency risk)
- Inflation structurally eliminated (fixed supply)
- **No hegemon:** Fair to all nations (unlike dollar dominance)

---

**Why This Could Work:**

✅ **Post-crisis demand for sound money** (historical pattern: after hyperinflation, populations reject fiat)  
✅ **Network effects** (40-60% already using = liquidity, infrastructure, familiarity)  
✅ **Neutrality** (no nation controls it = acceptable compromise in multipolar world)  
✅ **Technical feasibility** (Lightning Network, layer-2 solutions enable scale)  
✅ **Democratic pressure** (voters in democracies demand Bitcoin legalization post-crisis)  

---

**Why This Could Fail:**

❌ **Government resistance** (losing monetary control is existential threat)  
❌ **Volatility** (remains too high for reserve currency function)  
❌ **Coordination problem** (who moves first? First-mover risk)  
❌ **Technical limitations** (energy use, scaling, still unproven at global reserve scale)  
❌ **Authoritarian rejection** (China, Russia refuse to participate)  

---

#### Scenario 2: Multipolar Fragmentation (No Single Reserve)

**Thesis:** No single reserve currency emerges. Multiple regional blocs, Bitcoin as neutral settlement layer.

**The Logic:**

Geopolitical reality prevents single reserve:
- US dollar loses dominance (debt crisis destroys credibility)
- China yuan can't replace it (capital controls, authoritarian, regional only)
- Euro remains weak (structural issues, no fiscal union)
- **Result:** Multipolar world with Bitcoin as bridge

**How It Could Happen:**

**Regional Blocs Form:**
- Americas: Dollar (diminished but functional)
- Asia: Yuan (regional trade, not global)
- Europe: Euro (strengthened post-crisis but regional)
- Commodity exporters: Diversified baskets

**Bitcoin Becomes Settlement Layer:**
- No bloc trusts another's currency for reserves
- Bitcoin emerges as **neutral ground** (acceptable to all)
- International trade settles in BTC (instant, no intermediary)
- Domestic economies use fiat (government control), cross-border uses Bitcoin

**Historical Analog: Pre-1914 Multipolar System**
- British pound, French franc, German mark all functioned as reserves
- Gold was neutral settlement (what Bitcoin becomes in this scenario)
- Worked for decades until WWI disrupted coordination

---

**Why This Could Work:**

✅ **Reflects geopolitical reality** (multipolar world, no single hegemon)  
✅ **Bitcoin acceptable to all sides** (neutral, no political strings)  
✅ **Allows domestic monetary control** (governments keep fiat for local use)  
✅ **Pragmatic compromise** (doesn't require full Bitcoin standard)  

**Why This Could Fail:**

❌ **Coordination costs high** (multiple currencies = friction, complexity)  
❌ **Bitcoin volatility** (problematic for daily trade settlement)  
❌ **Nationalist resistance** (governments want full monetary control)  
❌ **Unstable equilibrium** (could collapse into Scenario 1 or 3)  

---

#### Scenario 3: CBDC Authoritarianism (Digital Fiat Persists)

**Thesis:** Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) maintain fiat dominance in authoritarian states. Democratic world adopts crypto. **Result: bifurcated global system.**

**The Logic:**

Crisis triggers divergent responses:
- **Authoritarian states** (China, Russia): Double down on control via CBDCs
- **Democratic states** (US, UK, EU): Legalize crypto under voter pressure
- **Result:** Two incompatible monetary systems (digital iron curtain)

**How It Could Happen:**

**Authoritarian CBDC Enforcement (2030-2035):**
- China mandates digital yuan, bans crypto entirely
- AI surveillance detects crypto transactions, harsh penalties
- Works domestically (total control) but causes brain drain
- Capital controls prevent outflows (underground markets suppressed)

**Democratic Crypto Adoption (2033-2036):**
- Western populations demand crypto legalization (vote with wallets)
- Governments capitulate (political survival requires it)
- Bitcoin legal tender in US, UK, EU by 2035
- Result: Democratic world on crypto, authoritarian world on CBDCs

**The Bifurcated World:**
- **Authoritarian bloc:** CBDC dominance (surveillance, control, closed systems)
- **Democratic bloc:** Crypto-friendly (Bitcoin reserves, open systems)
- **Trade between blocs:** Difficult (incompatible systems, trust deficit)
- **Capital flows:** One-way (democratic world attracts capital, authoritarian suffers brain drain)

---

**Why This Could Work:**

✅ **China proves enforcement CAN work** (2021 crypto ban succeeded domestically)  
✅ **CBDCs attractive to autocrats** (surveillance + control)  
✅ **Democratic voter pressure forces crypto adoption** (political necessity)  
✅ **Reflects ideological divide** (authoritarian vs democratic systems)  

**Why This Could Fail:**

❌ **Brain drain unsustainable** (talent/capital flee authoritarian bloc)  
❌ **Economic underperformance** (closed systems lose to open crypto economies)  
❌ **Underground markets persist** (even China can't fully suppress crypto)  
❌ **Eventually forced to adopt** (or fall further behind economically)  

---

### The SPICE Position: Agnostic on Endgame

**We don't know** which scenario prevails. Could be Bitcoin standard (Scenario 1). Could be multipolar fragmentation (Scenario 2). Could be CBDC authoritarianism in parts of the world (Scenario 3). Could be something entirely different that we haven't imagined.

**What we DO know:**

✓ **Current system is unsustainable:** Dollar reserve + Debt/GDP >175% + AI deflation = structural break  
✓ **Crisis window: 2029-2035:** Bond market break, hyperinflation/deflation collision, acute phase  
✓ **Hard assets outperform:** Historical law during reserve currency transitions  
✓ **Post-crisis order takes decades:** Stabilization period 2035-2050+  

**SPICE hedges the TRANSITION, not the endpoint.**

Whether Bitcoin becomes the global reserve (Scenario 1), or just a trade settlement layer (Scenario 2), or only dominates the democratic world (Scenario 3), **holding Bitcoin during the crisis (2029-2033) preserves wealth while fiat collapses.**

The post-crisis monetary order may take decades to crystallize—just as the dollar didn't fully replace sterling until the 1960s-70s (Bretton Woods 1944, but transition took 25+ years). SPICE investors exit when the acute crisis resolves (2034-2036), regardless of which monetary system ultimately emerges.

**We're not betting on the new world order—we're betting AGAINST the current one.**

### Why Bitcoin COULD Be the Reserve (Speculative)

**If pressed to assess probabilities** (beyond SPICE investment scope), Scenario 1 has compelling logic:

**The Fairness Argument:**
- Post-crisis world needs neutral reserve (no single hegemon)
- Dollar failed (US abused printing privilege → crisis)
- Yuan unacceptable (authoritarian, capital controls, regional only)
- Euro insufficient (structural weaknesses, regional only)
- Gold impractical (can't move digitally, settlement friction)
- **Bitcoin:** Digital, decentralized, **no one controls it** = only credible neutral option

**The Stability Argument:**
- Fixed supply (21M coin cap) prevents inflation structurally
- Can't be printed by any government (unlike all fiat currencies)
- Forces fiscal discipline (governments must tax/cut, not print)
- **Post-crisis populations DEMAND sound money** (historical pattern after hyperinflation)

**The Network Effects Argument:**
- By 2034, 40-60% of population already using Bitcoin (crisis adoption)
- First-mover advantage: liquidity, infrastructure, merchant acceptance, familiarity
- Hard to coordinate switch to alternative (path dependency)
- **Winner-take-most dynamics** (like TCP/IP, HTTP—network protocols exhibit monopoly tendencies)

**The Historical Precedent:**
- Gold standard (1870s) wasn't imposed by empire—countries **chose** it voluntarily
- Why? Credibility, attract capital, prevent debasement, facilitate trade
- Lasted 40+ years until WWI (governments suspended during wartime)
- **Bitcoin advantage:** No central authority to suspend (decentralized = harder to abandon)

**The Democratic Pressure:**
- After hyperinflation, populations vote for sound money (historical pattern)
- Opposition parties win elections on "legalize Bitcoin" platforms
- **Democracies forced to adopt** (political survival requires it)
- Authoritarian holdouts suffer brain drain, economic underperformance

**The Timeline:**
- 2034: First major economy adopts (likely US or UK post-election)
- 2035-2036: EU follows (political pressure, trade necessity)
- 2037-2040: Global cascade (trading partners adopt, network effects dominate)
- 2040+: Bitcoin as global reserve (fiat persists domestically but pegged to BTC)

---

**But this is speculation beyond SPICE scope.**

SPICE doesn't **require** Bitcoin to become the reserve currency. It only requires Bitcoin to **outperform during the crisis**—which ALL hard assets do during reserve currency transitions (historical law: Dutch→British, British→US).

### The Investment Thesis (Restated)

**SPICE is NOT a bet on Bitcoin as future reserve currency.**

**SPICE IS a hedge against the current reserve currency failing.**

| Scenario | SPICE Performance |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Scenario 1:** Bitcoin becomes global reserve | SPICE wins **big** (10-50× from crisis lows, secular bull market 2035+) |
| **Scenario 2:** Bitcoin becomes trade settlement layer | SPICE wins **medium** (5-15× from crisis lows, stable role 2035+) |
| **Scenario 3:** Bitcoin stays niche but democratic-world asset | SPICE wins **small** (3-8× from crisis lows, regional adoption) |
| **No crisis scenario:** Debt/GDP stabilizes, system doesn't break | SPICE **loses** (opportunity cost vs equities) |

**Key insight:** We don't need Scenario 1 to succeed. Even Scenario 3 (Bitcoin niche asset) delivers positive returns **because hard assets outperform fiat during reserve currency crises** (empirical law).

**SPICE models the crisis (2029-2035). What comes after is above our pay grade.**

We hedge the transition. The endpoint—whether Bitcoin standard, multipolar fragmentation, or bifurcated world—will become clear in the 2030s-2040s. SPICE investors exit when the acute crisis resolves (2034-2036), taking profits regardless of which long-term scenario unfolds.

**The only scenario that matters for SPICE:** Current system breaks (high probability). Hard assets preserve wealth during break (historical certainty). Post-crisis order crystallizes over decades (not our concern).
