Understanding Crisis Types
The SPICE framework identifies three historical crisis types based on reserve currency transitions over 500 years (Dutch → British → US). AI and crypto modify all three types by compressing timelines and introducing novel constraints on traditional policy tools.
1
Fast Collapse
Months to 2 years (AI/crypto: weeks to months)
Sudden loss of confidence triggers bank runs and hyperinflation. Government authority evaporates rapidly. Currency becomes worthless. Institutions fail. Digital acceleration: Social media panic, instant crypto flight, algorithmic trading amplify speed 2-3×.
Historical: Weimar Germany (1923), Zimbabwe (2008), Venezuela (2016-2020)
AI/Crypto analog: Stablecoin bank run (2025 scenario)
2
Slow Decline
50+ years (AI/crypto: 10-15 years)
Gradual loss of reserve status. Government manages decline through financial repression, capital controls, mild inflation. Institutions remain functional. Wages erode slowly. Digital acceleration: Crypto makes capital controls harder; timeline compresses 3-5×.
Historical: British Pound (1914-1971), Portuguese Escudo (1500s-1999), Ottoman Lira (1800s-1923)
AI/Crypto analog: CBDC-controlled slow fade (authoritarian path)
3
